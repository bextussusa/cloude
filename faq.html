Frequently Asked Questions

Your questions about multiplayer gaming, answered.

What is "cross-play" and why is it important?

Cross-play (or cross-platform play) is a feature that allows people using different gaming hardware—like a PC, PlayStation, Xbox, or Nintendo Switch—to play the same game together online. It's incredibly important because it breaks down the barriers between friends. If you have a PC but your friend has a PlayStation, cross-play lets you team up without anyone needing to buy new equipment. We always highlight when a game supports cross-play because it makes connecting so much easier.

How do you choose which games to feature?

Our selection process is centered on one thing: is this game genuinely fun to play with friends? We look for games with smooth gameplay, a clear purpose, and great social dynamics. We also prioritize titles with positive, non-toxic communities and fair business models. We're wary of games with aggressive "pay-to-win" mechanics. Every recommendation comes from hours of actual play-time, ensuring our reviews are authentic and trustworthy.

What's the difference between co-op and competitive multiplayer?

It's all about teamwork versus rivalry! In cooperative (co-op) games, you and your friends are on the same team, working together to overcome challenges, defeat AI-controlled enemies, or solve puzzles. Think of games where you're all fighting a dragon. In competitive games, you play against each other, either as individuals (free-for-all) or on opposing teams. This is where you test your skills against your friends to see who comes out on top.

Do I need a powerful gaming PC to enjoy these games?

Absolutely not! While some big-budget titles require high-end hardware, the world of multiplayer gaming is vast. Many of the most creative and fun experiences come from indie developers who design their games to run on a wide variety of systems, including older PCs and laptops. We also feature many browser-based and mobile games that require no special hardware at all. We make a point to mention system requirements so you know what to expect.

What is "couch co-op" or "local multiplayer"?

This is the classic way of playing together! "Couch co-op" or "local multiplayer" refers to playing a game with friends in the same physical room, on the same screen (often split-screen) or on the same device. It's perfect for parties and hangouts. Many modern games are online-only, so we love to highlight the fantastic local multiplayer games that keep this tradition alive.

How can I find people to play with if my friends are busy?

That's a common challenge! Many games have built-in matchmaking systems that will pair you with other players online. For a more community-focused approach, we recommend using platforms like Discord, which has thousands of servers dedicated to specific games or gaming in general. It's a great way to find like-minded players, join a group, and make new friends who share your passion.

How can I suggest a game for you to review?

We'd love to hear from you! Our community's suggestions are one of our best sources for discovering hidden gems. Please use the contact form on our home page to send us your ideas. Let us know what you love about the game and why you think it's a great fit for playing with friends. We can't promise to review every suggestion, but we read them all!
